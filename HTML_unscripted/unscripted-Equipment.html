  • Equipment Skills Discussion:

Equipment here refers to all of the various equipments required for production assembly and testing. Equipment design must provide the necessary functionalities to assist in assembly and assure the quality of a product offer. Equipment designs must produce tools that are ergonomic in use and protect operators from all potential hazards. Equipment designs must be robust, maintainable, accurate, meet production line takt times, provide total capacity, and respect space restrictions. All designs must meet their target costs. Equipment designs must comply with the regulatory agency mandates as with UL 508A, NFPA 79, and OSHA Nationally Recognized Test Lab (NRTL) approval.

Equipment designs are generally a combination of mechanical assemblies (tooling, fixturing, pneumatics, and structures) that are controlled by automation. The automation generally consists of a control program (PLC, LabVIEW, C++) and electronics (instrument clusters, sensors, motor drives, pneumatic manifolds, lasers). The offer is exclusively within the electronics category.
