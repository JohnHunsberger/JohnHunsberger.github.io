Capabilities offered and how they are ranked.

The information below presents the technology offer. The skills are organized into four categories. Each of the four categories is presented in more detail on a dedicated page. There is an explanation of how the skill offers are assigned a maturity rank based on a self assessment.

The capabilities presented are as skill based competencies. The competencies offered are grouped into four categories. Each category presents a list of associated skills. The categories are:

  • Project
  • Engineering
  • Product
  • Equipment

Project skills are those work skills offered that pertain to the collaboration process to plan, track and measure the various phases required of a project team to produce a specified result.

Engineering skills are those work skills offered that pertain to the collaborative efforts for conceptualization, architecture, detailed design, debug, test, and manufacture of a specified product offer.

Product skills are those work skills offered that pertain to the life cycle, agency compliance, safety, upgrade, maintenance, and support for marketed products.

Equipment skills are those work skills offered that pertain to the design of production test equipment for safety, robustness, capability and capacity.

The word 'competency' by itself does not include any specific measure1. Because it is not sufficient to only state that a capability exists, each skill presented also includes an indication of that skill's maturity. A three star system is used to convey a maturity level for each skill. The assignment of stars is based on a personal skills assessment. Consequently it is important that an interview take place to assure the fit of this service to a particular project requirement.

Rank Stars:

One star indicates that the skill is not core but has previously been performed during employment for an assigned task. (Exposure)
Two stars indicate that the skill is core and has been performed as required for assignments within the past five years. (Competent)
Three stars indicate that the skill is core and has been performed frequently for assignments within the past five years. (Strength)

In addition to specific skills, some employers attach a significance to the type of business environment in which a particular skill was applied. Summary:

The accumulated work experience presented spans a diverse range of competencies involving both electronic and software development. The software development in itself is diverse. It includes both high level application and low level embedded real-time controls. The commercial product experience includes plant control system, programmable logic controllers, product communication protocol accessories, line isolation monitor, electronic breaker test equipment and electronic voting machine. The aerospace product experience includes electronic and software development for satellite ground stations and testing of complex satellite components.

It is important to understand the applicability of the skill levels presented. A skill level offering may be too low-level for a required task and could rule this service out. That same skill level may be complete and sufficient to successfully provide a supporting role for that same task that could could help achieve a critical schedule milestone. The goal is to provide high quality engineering assistance that fills a resource gap for your offer process.
