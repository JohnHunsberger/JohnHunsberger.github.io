  • Product Concept Discussion:

It is expected that the best product design efforts result in a product that is safe, has least cost, achieves robust implementation of the specification's functionality/performance, and keeps the development costs within the project budget. It is also expected that existing product offers continue to be available for sale without disruptions due to component obsolescence or supplier issues. Safety has always been a paramount concern in all design activity.

During the concept phase of a new product offer the deliverables serve to determine whether the product offer has market viability with respect to the proposed sell price. At first a number of design proposals are prepared based on the preliminary marketing product specification1. A technical review process assures the feasibility of the requirements and selects one of the proposed implementations. That design concept becomes the basis for an estimation of the product and project costs. Projected product costs have been created for circuit boards, firmware and software projects. Estimations are created by either analysis or comparison. In previous analysis estimations the costs factors have included total functionality, component costs, design complexity, required performance limits and producibility costs of labor and plant. A separate presentation of project related skills is available in "Project". Please review that information regarding specific project skills offered.

  • Product Design Discussion:

During the design phase the product design deliverables start with a design specification1. The design activity is to produce a product that implements precisely the functionality and performance defined in the product specification. The design competencies offered are subdivided into two areas; Electrical and Software design.

Electrical design experience includes digital circuitry, microcontrollers and analog interfaces. Some typical design factors addressed are timing, propagation delays, currents, power dissipation and noise. Software deliverables have included real time control assembly level code and high level application code. Design of embedded software has addressed such design factors as tool chain, execution speed, memory allocation, interrupts, timers, A/D, code/data separation, stack management, register usage and in-circuit debugging.

Design of high level software has addressed design factors such as Operating System, IDE, version control, configuration control, architecture, user interface, security, design patterns, data structures, data types, scope, thread safety, performance, libraries, APIs, DLLs and robustness.

All design work has had to be qualified before being released to manufacturing. Qualification test plans varied between electrical and software designs. Both types of design work have agency requirements that apply. In general a compliance to MIL, FCC, NFPA, NEC, IEC, UL, CE and IEC sets design implementation requirements and restrictions for the purpose of consumer safety or systems compatibility. Many of the past product modifications performed due to obsolescence required a UL inspection and update of the UL report on file. One example of past effort was the preparation of the Shoup Electronic Voting Machines for certification by the Federal Election Commission. This required the preparation of the firmware and software to conform with the Voting Systems Standards3 that reference the ANSI/ ISO/ ASQC QS9000-3-1997. The actual testing was performed by Wyle Labs in Huntsville, Al., now NTS.

All manufactured product designs have been designed with regard to manufacturability. Specifications for specific properties of the product assemblies that must be tested and verified at manufacture have been supplied to the manufacturing quality assurance. The additional section of Equipment contains detail regarding the preparation of production assembly and test equipment. Also, a separate presentation of design related skills is available in "Engineering". Please review that information regarding specific engineering skills offered.

  • Product Support Discussion:

During the support phase the product function or analysis has frequently required direct customer contact. Customer satisfaction has been considered to be paramount to the successful launch of any new product, whether it was an electronic or software design. There is an understanding of the urgency by the customer to have their issues resolved in a timely manner. Providing the necessary engineering skills to resolve customer issues assures them that their business is valued.

Product offers in the later stage of their life cycle have component obsolescence issues. It has been necessary to make design changes to accommodate new components. Product change impacts product performance, product cost, manufacturing process, purchased parts, product testing and agency approvals (UL, CSA, IEC). The importance to maintain safety, quality, agency approvals, and producibility in any product change is understood.
